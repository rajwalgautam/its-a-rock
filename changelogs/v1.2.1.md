# v1.2.1

Read your beta more easily. The route planner can now zoom, and earlier moves get out of the way so you can actually see the holds.

## What's new

**Route planner**
- **Pinch to zoom** — pinch the wall photo to zoom in on tightly packed holds, and double-tap to snap back to fit.
- **Two-finger pan** — once zoomed in, drag with two fingers to move around the photo. One finger still places and nudges markers, so panning never gets in the way of editing.
- **Markers get out of the way** — as you build a sequence, each earlier move shrinks to a small numbered dot so it stops covering the hold underneath. The most recent move stays full-size, and tapping any dot (or selecting it in the move list) expands it again so you can fine-tune it.

## Under the hood

- `PlanCanvas` wraps the photo and marker overlay in a single reanimated transform layer (`transformOrigin: 'top left'`) so the zoom/pan is a plain affine; container taps invert the transform to image space, and the gesture surface stays untransformed for stable touch coordinates.
- Pinch zoom is focal-point anchored and clamped to `[1, 4]×`; translation is clamped each frame so the content can't be panned off the container.
- `LimbMarker` drag deltas are divided by the live `scale` shared value so dragging tracks the finger 1:1 at any zoom, and the new `compact` variant renders the small numbered dot.
- Edit-mode markers are full only when latest or selected (`dot: i !== moves.length - 1 && m.key !== selectedKey`); playback markers are unchanged.
